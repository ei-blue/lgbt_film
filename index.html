Looking at LGBTQ+ representation in the history of movie production, we are making progress, albeit imperfect...

“The Moral Guidelines”

The Hays Code, effective from 1934 to 1968, placed a series of censorship guidelines on movie production. Homosexuality was considered not acceptable.

1934

A Milestone in Queer Cinema

“The Boys in the Band”, centers on a gay friend group, is among the first major American motion pictures to revolve around gay characters.

1970

Starring Tom Hanks

A-list actor Tom Hanks won an Oscar for his portrayal of a gay lawyer in “Philadelphia”. The performance and the fame of Hanks helped broaden the understanding of AIDS.

1993

Into the Mainstream

Ang Lee’s “Brokeback Mountain” was a huge commercial success and won three Oscars, proving to Hollywood that LGBTQ+ stories had a place outside of strictly independent filmmaking.

2005

Intersectional Representation

“Moonlight”, the Best Picture winner at the 2017 Academy Awards, was one big step toward making gay cinema that isn’t whitewashed and features a range of identities.

2017

Our recommendations to the movie industry for the decades to come...

Including LGBTQ+ communities in movies shouldn't just be tokenism. The quantity of LGBTQ+ characters is never the best indication of true representation.

As with everybody else, the LGBTQ+ communities are just people – they are diverse, unique, and full of stories. Movies should portray people for who they are...and not who we want them to be.

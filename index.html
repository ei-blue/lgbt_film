Looking at LGBTQ+ representation in the movie history, we are making progress, albeit imperfect...

The Hays Code set stern guidelines for what types of content could be released by U.S. movie studios from 1934 to 1968. One taboo was homosexuality.

1934

The Boys in the Band

"The Boys in the Band" centers on a gay friend group. It is among the first major American motion pictures to revolve around gay characters and is often cited as a milestone in the history of queer cinema.

1970

Philadelphia

A-list actor Tom Hanks won an Oscar for his portrayal of a lawyer who's fired for having AIDS in Jonathan Demme's "Philadelphia," helping to dispel the myth that taking on LGBTQ+ roles would ruin straight actors’ careers.

1993

Brokeback Mountain

Ang Lee's "Brokeback Mountain" became a critical and box office success and won three Oscars, proving to Hollywood that LGBTQ+ stories had a place outside of strictly independent filmmaking.

2005

Moonlight

"Moonlight", the Best Picture winner at the 2017 Academy Awards, was one big step toward making gay cinema that isn't whitewashed, features a range of identities, and doesn't make its characters vehicles of suffering.

2017
